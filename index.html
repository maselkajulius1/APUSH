Gulf of Tonkin Resolution

In 1965, the United states invaded Vietnam in its quest to contain communism, using the justification of the new Gulf of Tonkin Resolution, based on a North Vietnamese attack in 1964 which was falsely construed by the NSA (National Security Agency). The United States vessel Maddox fired warning shots on three approaching North Vietnamese torpedo boats while performing an intelligence patrol and faced retaliation relatively unscathed, resulting in the death of 4 North Vietnamese individuals. The vessel reported that two days later, on August 4th, North Vietnamese boats attacked Maddox. Some doubts were cast on the event as soon as it happened, and it was later confirmed in 2003 by former secretary of defense Robert S. McNamara that no North Vietnamese vessels were present. The Gulf of Tonkin Resolution gave President Lyndon B. Johnson clearance to give military aid any country in South East Asia that was deemed jeopardized by the threat of communism.

Vietnam War

The Vietnam war quickly grew highly controversial and faced heavy backlash from the nations youth. Dubbed the "Television War", it was the first time in history US citizens could see the atrocities of war from their homes with the advent of the common household television. In 1965, when US troops were deployed, the war immediately saw backlash from youth activists and college students. By 1968, increased deployment and threats of the draft had driven the controversy to become a mainstream issue. This was also the year the Tet Offensive occurred, a major escalation and surprise attack from the Viet Cong against South Vietnamese and United States troops, which convinced many Americans that this was not a winning war for the country. By 1972 US involvement was waning, and by 1973 the war officially ended with the Paris Peace Accords. The consequences of this war was a death total between 1.32 million and 3.43 million, including 58,220 Americans. Americans were acutely aware of the atrocities committed by both sides including mass civilian killings, rape, torture, and guerilla warfare.
